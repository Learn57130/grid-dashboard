grid_strike guide
hyperliquid ◀ builder

A grid is a ladder of buy orders across a price range. Each rung buys when price dips to it and sells a little higher — harvesting the up-and-down chop. It profits while price stays inside the range; its risk is price trending out, leaving you holding a losing position.

anatomy of a grid

● buy when price dips to a rung  ·  ● sell a rung higher  ·  the limit halts it if price falls through the floor.

levels  = total_amount ÷ min_order  (capital limit — usually what binds)
spacing = range ÷ (levels − 1)   where range = (end − start) / start

More/tighter rungs? Raise total_amount or lower min_order. Wider rungs? Widen the range.

range where the grid sits

start_price low
Bottom rung. Set it below the current price.tune: drag the line on the chart, or use the ±% buttons around a center price.
end_price high
Top rung. Must be above start_price. Wider gap = bigger range = wider spacing for the same level count.tune: bracket where you expect price to oscillate. Too wide and rungs sit too far apart to fill.
limit_price stop
Invalidation. Below start_price. If price breaks below it, the grid halts — your downside cap.tune: set it where your thesis is wrong. Draggable on the chart.

sizing how much, how many rungs

total_amount_quote USDC
Total capital the grid deploys, split across rungs.tune: raise for more levels. At 1× leverage this is also the margin used.
min_order_amount_quote USDC
Notional per rung. Hyperliquid minimum ~$10.tune: lower for more levels from the same capital (down to ~$10).
min_spread_between_orders frac
Floor on spacing (0.001 = 0.1%). Rungs can't get closer than this.tune: rarely the binding limit — leave at 0.001.

take profit profit per rung

How far above its buy each rung sells. Drag the target and watch where the sell lands relative to the next rung:

The coherence rule. For a real grid, take_profit ≈ spacing — each rung sells at the next rung up. Far below spacing (0.15% vs ~2%), you're not gridding, just scalping a rung, and the wide spacing is cosmetic. The ⛓ link in the builder keeps them equal by default.

max_open_orders
Rungs resting at once. Default 2 = a sliding window near price. Set = level count to arm the full ladder.
max_orders_per_batch
Orders placed per round, nearest first. Raise to arm faster.
order_frequency s
Min seconds between rounds. Only bites while filling empty slots; does nothing once the ladder is full.
activation_bounds blank=null
Only place orders within this fraction of mid-price. Blank = always active across the range.
keep_position
On stop, keep the accumulated position instead of closing it.

economics estimate only — not written to config

cost/cycle = 2 × maker_fee + funding × hold_hrs  (≈ 0.03% fee + carry)
net/cycle  = take_profit − cost/cycle
maker_fee %
Your maker fee. HL perp base ≈ 0.015%. Both grid legs are LIMIT_MAKER, so a round trip ≈ 2×.
funding_hr %/hr
Hourly perp carry. Auto-filled live; positive = longs pay.
hold_hrs
Average hours a rung's position stays open — scales funding cost per cycle.
balance USDC
Account equity, for the margin-use estimate.

risk what can go wrong

A grid earns many small wins and risks one big loss: if price trends out of the range you keep buying into the fall, accumulating a position that bleeds down to the limit.

trend-down worst case — the tail

The grind (small take-profit wins) lives near the top; this curve is the unrealized loss you carry if price just walks down. On a perp, funding also bleeds a position held too long. The builder plots this live for your exact config.

safety before real money

Use an api_wallet key, never your main private key. The agent wallet from app.hyperliquid.xyz/API can trade but cannot withdraw — a leaked key can't drain funds. The builder only needs your public address for read-only balance.
  1. Keep leverage 1× — liquidation is remote.
  2. Fund the perp account: transfer USDC spot → perp in the HL app (BTC lives on the perp).
  3. Watch a grid succeed and fail for free first — paper trade or Hyperliquid testnet.
  4. Size it as tuition you can fully lose.
